Interesting Facts


Paris

The Eiffel Tower in Paris was originally intended to be a temporary installation for the 1889 World's Fair. It was almost torn down in 1909, but was saved because it proved to be a valuable communications tool.


London

London is one of the smallest capital cities in the world, covering an area of just 607 square miles. Despite its small size, it has a population of over 8 million people, making it the most populous city in the European Union.


Antarctica

Antarctica is the driest and coldest continent on Earth. It is also the windiest, with wind speeds reaching up to 200 miles per hour. Despite these extreme conditions, many species of animals and plants have adapted to survive in this harsh environment.


Greece

The Greek alphabet is the oldest known alphabet in the Western world. It was developed around 1000 BC and was based on the Phoenician alphabet. The Greek alphabet has 24 letters, and many of these letters are still used in mathematics, science, and other fields today.
